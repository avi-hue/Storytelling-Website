The Titanic, a marvel of engineering, was built in Belfast, Ireland, by the White Star Line. It was designed to be the largest and most luxurious ship of its time, with cutting-edge safety features.

2. The "Unsinkable" Ship

The Titanic was widely advertised as "practically unsinkable," due to its watertight compartments. People believed it to be the safest ship ever built.

3. Boarding the Titanic (April 10, 1912)

On the morning of April 10, 1912, thousands gathered to witness the Titanic's departure. 2,224 people boarded, including some of the world's wealthiest individuals and poor immigrants seeking a new life in America.

4. Setting Sail

The Titanic set sail at noon, making stops in Cherbourg, France, and Queenstown, Ireland, before heading into the open Atlantic.

First-class passengers enjoyed gourmet meals, heated swimming pools, and luxurious suites, while third-class passengers lived in crowded yet comfortable quarters.

6. Ice Warnings Begin (April 14, 1912)

Several ships, including the Californian and Mesaba, sent iceberg warnings, but the messages were not taken seriously.

Frederick Fleet, the lookout, suddenly saw a giant iceberg ahead. He rang the warning bell and shouted: "ICEBERG, RIGHT AHEAD!"

9. Desperate Maneuvers

The ship tried to turn, but it was too big to maneuver quickly. The iceberg scraped along its right side, tearing a 300-foot gash below the waterline.

11. Captain Smith Assesses the Situation

The ship's designer, Thomas Andrews, inspected the damage and told Captain Smith: "She will sink in less than two hours."

12. First-Class Passengers Unconcerned

Many first-class passengers dismissed the warnings, assuming the ship was still safe. Some even laughed at the idea of sinking.

13. The First Lifeboats Are Launched

At 12:20 AM, Captain Smith ordered the crew to begin evacuations, but people were hesitant to board lifeboats, thinking they were safer on the ship.

14. Third-Class Passengers Struggle

Many third-class passengers were trapped behind locked gates, struggling to find a way to the upper decks.

15. The Orchestra Plays

The Titanic's orchestra played music to keep passengers calm, even as the ship tilted forward.

16. Full Panic Breaks Out

As water reached the upper decks, people pushed and shoved to get onto lifeboats. Gunshots were fired to control the chaos.

17. The Titanic's Last Lights (2:10 AM)

The ship's electricity failed, and everything went dark. The stern rose into the air, while passengers held on desperately.

18. The Titanic Breaks in Half (2:18 AM)

The massive strain on the ship's structure caused it to snap in half. The bow sank first, followed by the stern minutes later.

19. Hundreds Struggle in the Freezing Water

Over 1,500 people were thrown into the freezing water. Most died within minutes due to hypothermia.

20. The Carpathia Arrives

At 4:00 AM, the RMS Carpathia arrived and rescued the 705 survivors who had escaped in lifeboats.

21. The World Reacts

The world was shocked by the tragedy. Many questioned why there were not enough lifeboats.

22. Investigations Begin

Governments launched investigations, leading to new maritime safety laws, including enough lifeboats for all passengers.

23. The Titanic's Wreck Is Discovered (1985)

In 1985, Dr. Robert Ballard found the Titanic's remains 12,500 feet below the sea, offering the world its first glimpse of the lost ship.

24. Titanic in Pop Culture

The story of the Titanic became legendary, inspiring books, films, and documentaries, keeping its memory alive.

25. A Legacy Remembered

The Titanic remains a symbol of human ambition and tragedy, reminding us of the power of nature and the cost of hubris.
